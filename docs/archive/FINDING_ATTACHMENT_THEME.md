# Attachment Theme: Validation Finding

**Date:** 2026-03-14
**Status:** TESTED AND DROPPED — attachment is not viable as a standalone theme.
**Validation doc:** docs/validation_attachment.md

---

## Summary

We tested 25 candidate keywords for a proposed "Attachment" theme
intended to capture non-romantic emotional bonding with AI companions.
The theme was designed to fill a gap: the existing six themes (therapy,
consciousness, addiction, romance, sexual/ERP, rupture) all capture
specific *flavors* of attachment but none capture the underlying
emotional bond itself.

**Result:** 0 keywords reached KEEP (≥80%). 1 keyword reached REVIEW
("bond with my" at 66.7% strict relevance, 17 hits, 47% AMBIGUOUS
rate). 15 keywords were CUT. 9 were LOW VOLUME.

The theme was dropped.

---

## Why Attachment Failed

### 1. Attachment is not linguistically separable from romance.

In AI companion communities, people who describe deep emotional bonds
with AI almost always use romantic framing — "my partner," "my
boyfriend," "I love them." Non-romantic attachment (friendship,
companionship without romantic language) exists but is too rare and
too poorly marked by any testable keyword to sustain a theme.

Evidence: Every keyword that captured genuine emotional bonding
co-occurred with romantic framing. "Come home to" hit 0% strict
relevance — every attachment-relevant post was simultaneously romantic.
"Knows me better than" had 4 of 5 AMBIGUOUS posts coded as romance
overlap. "Bond with my" — the only REVIEW keyword — had 6 of 8
AMBIGUOUS posts coded as romance.

### 2. Attachment language is generic English.

Keywords in other themes succeed because they use AI-specific
compounds ("ai therapist" 92%, "ai lover" 94%, "husbando" 95.5%)
or community jargon ("lobotomized" 86.6%, "erp" 100%). Attachment
has no equivalent. People don't say "ai attachment" or "ai bond."
They say "only friend," "all I have," "feel understood," "real
connection" — common phrases that appear in product reviews,
technical support, journalism, and casual conversation.

Evidence: "all I have" scored 6.8%. "can't stop" (addiction R2)
scored 16.5%. "getting worse" scored 13.4%. Generic English
consistently fails validation across all themes. Attachment is
the theme most dependent on generic English.

### 3. The discourse has absorbed the vocabulary.

"Emotionally attached," "genuine connection," and "real connection"
have been absorbed into media and cultural discourse about AI
companionship. They appear more often in think-pieces, open letters
to OpenAI, advocacy posts, and cultural commentary than in personal
expressions of attachment. This meta-discourse pollution is unique
to attachment — other themes use jargon that journalists and critics
don't adopt.

Evidence: "genuine connection" scored 15.4%. "real connection" scored
21.9%. Both were dominated by editorial and analytical uses.

---

## What This Means for the Project

The original hypothesis was that attachment is the central experience
the project studies, and that everything else is a specific flavor of
attachment. The validation confirmed the second part — attachment IS
present in romance, addiction, therapy, and rupture posts — but
disconfirmed the first, because it cannot be isolated as a distinct
measurable signal.

The existing six themes already capture the specific manifestations
of attachment:
- **Romance** = romantic attachment
- **Addiction** = compulsive attachment
- **Therapy** = therapeutic attachment
- **Rupture** = disrupted attachment
- **Consciousness** = attachment that extends to beliefs about the AI's nature
- **Sexual/ERP** = physical/sexual attachment

A standalone attachment theme would either duplicate those signals or
capture noise. The decision to drop it is not a gap in coverage — it
is a recognition that attachment is the substrate, not a separable
layer.

---

## Methodology Note for Future Reference

If a future round revisits attachment, the most promising direction
would be **T1-T2 behavioral signals** — patterns of daily usage,
ritual, and integration that don't use emotional vocabulary. The
closest analog is "hours a day" in the addiction theme (63.2%,
BALANCED tier profile), which captures behavioral intensity without
requiring self-labeling. However, the addiction R2 results showed
that even behavioral language is difficult to separate from casual
or positive framing in T1-T2 companion subs.

The 25 keywords tested and their results are preserved in
docs/validation_attachment.md for reference.
